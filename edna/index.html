About

Welcome,

I’m Edna Shindinde, personal style advisor & wardrobe editor.

I help women embrace their value by teaching how to build a staple wardrobe in line with their inner power and beauty.

The greatest flair of mine is to see talent and strength behind every woman I meet. Whether you build a career, take care of a family, own a business or enjoy life by your rules – you are a high-value woman. You set a precedent & should own it proudly!

My vision is for each women to unblushingly celebrate her beauty, to be seen and respected without doubting own looks. Styling is just one way to achieve that. And I couldn’t ask for a better mission, as to guide you on this journey.

Knitwear blend guide
Looking to upgrade your sweater collection this fall? Us, too; and this year, we’re even more interested than ever in what our favorite styles of sweaters are made of and how that affects their practicality. A sweater material can change the whole experience, so it’s important to know what you are looking for in a sweater and which material can help you best meet your vision. Sweater materials can seem quite complicated, which is why we have put together this handy guide to help all your sweater dreams come true.
Sweater materials can seem quite complicated
When peeking at the tag on the inside of your sweater, you'll find the specific material, whether it be 100% cashmere or multiple materials such as 70% wool, 20% acrylic, and 10% cashmere.
Wool
Wool is the most common material for sweaters and can refer to a variety of animal fibers. This natural material is perfect for function, style, and comfort. Wools are also often long-lasting and high-quality, meaning you will get to enjoy their fabulous fuzziness for years to come. Wool can include sheep’s wool, rabbit’s angora, alpaca, and other natural animal-based fibers. These animals are typically bred and raised for their coats, which are humanely sheared once they’ve reached a certain length to be woven into thread for clothing and other uses.
These animals are typically bred and raised for their coats
One of the most commonly used wool in sweaters is Merino sheep’s wool. Merino wool is known for being hydrophilic, meaning it can absorb up to 30% of its weight in water, making it a great weatherproof choice of sweater. Women’s wool sweaters, including cashmere, can be made from a mix of materials. This can make the wool less itchy and more comfortable to wear. If you prefer a pure wool sweater, consider wearing a thermal or shirt underneath to offset any potential itching, though that itchiness typically subsides after a few wears and washes.
Linen clothes
The Benefits of Linen Clothes
Linen clothes are a popular choice for many people due to their many benefits. Firstly, linen is a breathable material that allows air to circulate around the body, making it ideal for hot and humid climates. This natural fabric is also hypoallergenic, making it suitable for those with sensitive skin.
Another advantage of linen clothes is that they are incredibly durable and long-lasting. Linen fibers are up to three times stronger than cotton fibers, which means that linen clothes are less likely to tear or wear out over time. Additionally, linen fabric softens with each wash and becomes more comfortable to wear over time.
Linen
Linen clothes also have an attractive texture and drape beautifully. They have a relaxed, effortless style that is perfect for casual wear. Linen shirts, pants, and dresses are versatile and can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion. They are also easy to care for, as most linen garments can be machine washed and dried.

Lastly, choosing linen clothes is a sustainable choice for your wardrobe. Linen is made from the fibers of the flax plant, which is a renewable resource. It also requires less water and energy to produce than other fabrics like cotton or polyester, making it an eco-friendly option.

In conclusion, if you are looking for a comfortable, durable, and sustainable fabric for your wardrobe, linen is an excellent choice. With its natural breathability, hypoallergenic properties, and effortless style, linen clothes are a versatile and timeless addition to any wardrobe.
